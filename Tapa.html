Tapsilog

Good for 4

Tapa

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
1 Ib sirloin (thinly sliced against the grain into bite-size strips)
6 pcs garlic cloves (coarsely chopped)
1/3 cup light brown sugar (packed)
1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
1 tbsp distilled white vinegar
1 tbsp cornstarch
1/2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
1/4 tsp kosher salt
1/4 cup vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Pat beef dry with paper towels.
  2. Whisk garlic, brown sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, cornstarch, red pepper flakes, black pepper, and salt in a medium bowl.
  3. Add beef to marinade and toss well to coat. Cover and chill at least 6 hours and up to 1 day.
  4. Heat oil in a large nonstick skillet over high.
  5. Drain beef in a sieve, letting as much excess marinade drip off as possible.
  6. Add half of beef to skillet, arranging in a single layer and cook, turning halfway through, until beef is cooked through and deeply browned in spots, about 3 minutes (be careful; the marinade will splatter). Transfer beef to a plate.
  7. Discard any garlic remaining in pan and repeat process with remaining beef.
  8. Keep warm. Clean skillet and set aside.

Tapsilog Assembly

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
4 cups chilled leftover cooked jasmine rice
1/2 tsp (plus more) Morton kosher salt
1/4 cup (or more) vegetable oil
12 pcs garlic cloves (finely chopped)
4 pcs large egg

Instructions

  1. Place rice in a large bowl and break up with a rubber spatula. Stir in 1 tsp. Diamond Crystal or Â½ tsp. Morton kosher salt; set aside.
  2. Heat oil in reserved skillet over medium-low. Cook garlic, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and crisp, about 5 minutes.
  3. Drain garlic in a fine-mesh sieve set over a small bowl; transfer to a small plate.
  4. Pour 2 Tbsp. garlic oil back into skillet and heat over medium-high. Add reserved rice mixture and stir to coat in oil. Cook, stirring occasionally, until heated through, about 5 minutes. Set 1 Tbsp. fried garlic aside for serving and stir remaining fried garlic into rice. Transfer to a bowl and keep warm.
  5. Heat remaining garlic oil in the same skillet over medium-high (you should have 2 Tbsp.; add vegetable oil to make up the difference if needed). Crack eggs into skillet and season with salt. Cook until whites are almost set, about 2 minutes. Carefully tilt skillet so oil pools on one side. Spoon oil over egg whites until fully set, about 1 minute.
  6. Divide rice mixture, meat, and eggs among plates and top with reserved fried garlic.
