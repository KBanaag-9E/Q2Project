Halo Halo

Good for 4

Minatamis Na Saging (sweetened bananas) and Tapioca

Ingredients

  • 3 saba bananas or 2 regular bananas
  • 1 cup water, plus more for the pearls
  • ¾ cup packed light brown sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: ¼ cup small tapioca pearls

Instructions

  1. Optional: Bring a medium saucepan of water to a boil over medium-high heat. Add 1/4 cup small tapioca pearls, stir to combine, and reduce the heat to maintain a simmer. Simmer until the tapioca is al dente (translucent with small white centers), about 7 to 8 minutes. Drain through a fine-mesh strainer.
  2. Peel and slice 3 saba or 2 regular bananas crosswise into 1/4-inch-thick rounds.
  3. Place 1 cup water, 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt in a large saucepan. Stir and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to maintain a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the bananas and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to maintain a low simmer and cook until tender and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract and tapioca if using, and stir to combine, breaking up any clumps. Transfer to a medium bowl and let cool to room temperature. Cover and refrigerate until cold, about 2 hours.

Halo Halo Assembly

Ingredients

  • 1 (20-ounce) can jackfruit in syrup
  • ⅔ cup canned full-fat unsweetened coconut milk
  • 3 tablespoons evaporated milk
  • 3 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk
  • Minatamis na saging and tapioca (recipe above)
  • 4 scoops coconut or ube ice cream
  • ½ cup macapuno (coconut sport strings)
  • ½ cup ube halaya
  • 1½ cups nata de coco
  • 4 cups shaved ice, plus more

Instructions

  1. Remove the jackfruit from a 20-ounce can, leaving the syrup behind. Coarsely chop the jackfruit into bite-sized pieces. Return the jackfruit back to the can.
  2. Place ⅔ cup coconut milk, 3 tablespoons evaporated milk, and 3 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk in a small bowl or liquid measuring cup and stir to combine.
  3. For each serving, layer the ingredients in a 16-ounce glass in the following order:
    1. ⅓ cup minatamis na saging
    2. 1 scoop coconut or ube ice cream
    3. 2 tablespoons macapuno
    4. 2 tablespoons ube halaya
    5. ¼ cup jackfruit and syrup
    6. ⅓ cup coco de nata
    7. Top with 1 cup shaved ice and drizzle with 3 tablespoons of the milk mixture.
    8. Add more shaved ice to fill the glass if needed.
  4. Serve with a long spoon and mix it all up before eating.
